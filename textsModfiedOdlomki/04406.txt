The Explorer was not  comfortable in the alien air. It felt as thick as soup and he had to  breathe shallowly. Even so--    He reached out in a sudden need for company. The Merchant was warm to  the touch. His breathing was rough, he moved in an occasional spasm, and  was obviously asleep. The Explorer hesitated and decided not to wake  him. It would serve no real purpose.    There would be no rescue, of course. That was the penalty paid for the  high profits which unrestrained competition could lead to.
